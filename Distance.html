Distance Estimation

Scenario: in a top-down (overhead) camera view, estimate whether the distance between vehicles and construction machinery is safe. The workflow includes object detection (vehicles/personnel/equipment) and distance computation for collision-risk monitoring.

Why it matters

  • Collision-risk prevention: continuously monitoring proximity between moving vehicles and heavy equipment helps reduce struck-by and equipment collision incidents.
  • Real-time safety alerts: if estimated distances fall below a threshold, the system can trigger warnings for supervisors or operators.
  • Low infrastructure overhead: a single overhead camera can cover a wide area and support site-wide proximity analytics.

Figure: Proximity monitoring to reduce equipment collision risk in construction environments.

Method overview

Part 1 — Object detection + pixel distance

First, we perform object detection (bounding boxes) to identify vehicles/personnel/equipment at the construction site. For each detected object, we compute a center point (centroid) and then calculate the distance between centroids in pixel space.

Figure: Detect objects, compute their centroids, and measure center-to-center distance in pixels.

Part 2 — Angle correction + scale calibration (pixel → meters)

Next, we obtain pixel coordinates in the image and conduct scale calibration to convert pixel distance into real-world distance (meters). Since the view can be tilted (not perfectly orthographic), we apply an angle-modified Ground Sampling Distance (GSD) to correct for perspective effects. 

Obtain their pixel coordinates in the image.
Then, conduct scale calibration (converting 'pixel distance' to 'meters').

D = sqrt( (GSDx * (x1 - x2))^2 + (GSDy * (y1 - y2))^2 )

GSDx = (H * f * cosθ) / (-dy * sinθ + f * cosθ)^2 * d
GSDy = (H * f)       / (-dy * sinθ + f * cosθ)^2 * d

Where:
- GSD is Ground Sampling Distance after angle modified
- H is the height of the camera
- θ is the rotation angle
- d is a pixel distance
- f is the focal length

Strengths

  • Single-camera solution: only an overhead camera is needed to estimate distances over a large area.
  • Actionable safety metric: distance thresholds can be tied directly to site rules for near-miss monitoring and alarms.
  • Angle-aware scaling: the GSD-based correction helps reduce bias introduced by camera tilt (compared to raw pixel distance).

Limitations

  • Accuracy is sensitive to imaging conditions: distance estimates are affected by camera resolution, top-down angle/tilt, and detection accuracy.
  • Calibration dependency: errors in camera height, rotation angle, or focal length propagate directly into meter-level distance estimates.
  • Occlusion and box jitter: partial occlusion and unstable bounding boxes can cause noisy centroids and fluctuating distance outputs.
